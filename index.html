    • A Little About my Art

    The first thing I knew how to do when coming into this program was illustrate. I've been illustrating ever since I was able to hold a pencil, inspired by the little doodles of people my father had made, wanting to be just like him. I seriously started getting into it in middle school, teaching myself everything I know now. Since then, I've developed a good style for myself. The majority of my art is done in a cartoony style, but lately I've been challenging myself to do more than just that. I've been able to learn a lot about my art and myself in these classes.

    Why Graphic Design?

    Originally, I had wanted to be an art teacher, but designing my boyfriend's food truck had sparked my growing passion for graphic design. After a major change, this program helped me see that this is exactly where I need to be. Though I aspired to be strictly an illustrator at first, this program allowed me to challenge myself, and I was able to see I can do so much more than just create illustrations. I never thought I'd be able to make things like calendars, books, or even really bad horror short films. I can see myself having a career in this field. I wish I figured this out BEFORE being one semester away from graduating, but I wouldn't have the friends I made.

    What's the Plan After Graduation?

    Before ANYTHING, I've been going to school for the last 16 years of my life. I'm taking a little bit of time to catch up on all of the sleep I had lost as a graduation present for myself. After that, I really wish to do freelance design one day. But before that, I'm aware of how it could be difficult to make it if you dive right into freelance, so I plan to get a job somewhere to get experience, and my foot in the door of the graphic designer world. As much as I don't want to work for the man, I'll have to if I want to start making a name for myself. It's nice to see familiar faces in this class again!
